Phoenix One

Beachside Campfire

  • Beachside C

    Developed with: G27

    Released: 2023

    Play it here!

    A unique VR experience designed to assist a user in getting ready for sleep.

    Relax and doze off as you "watch" the comforting lightshow of a beach-side campfire in front of you, listening to a carefully constructed sleep-inducing soundscape.

  • My Responsibilities

    Narrative Designer

    I was responsible for designing and writing the narrative that would be spoken by the voice actors to help induce a feeling that focussed on encouraging sleep.

    Audio Engineer

    I was also responsible for recording, mixing and implementing various audio tracks that combined together to create an audioscape which enhanced the realism of the experience, as well as contributed towards helping the user get ready for sleep.

  • My Contributions

    • Calming Sounds

      With the largest part of this project, the visuals, being done by other teammates, I was assigned the second-largest part, which was the audio and the soundscape that would be created to help the user get ready for sleep.

      The audio had to be created in a way that allowed the user to feel comfortable and relaxed, but also prevented them from focussing too much on the noise rather than trying to sleep, which meaning recording and mixing sounds that both had to be quiet and non-intrusive, such as ocean noises without the crashing of waves, fire sounds without the peaking and crackling of the fire, etc.

      Similarly to the rest of the sounds, the narration and chatting from the other "people" had to be at a very precise volume, as the narration and chatting had to be loud enough for the user to register that they were surrounded by other people, but quiet enough so that what was being said could not be determined and the user would focus on the speech rather than trying to feel sleepy.

      Given how this was a VR experience that was set on a remote beach, I felt that narration by others would enhance the experience even more by making the user feel "safe" in their imaginary environment, knowing that there were "people" situated around them.
